Royyala Igguru

South Indian Prawn Curry

Total Calories: 250 calories
Ingredients:
  • 500g prawns (cleaned & deveined) - Fresh prawns work best for this dish as they absorb the flavors well.
    		•
  • 2 onions, finely chopped - Adds a rich base and sweetness to balance the spices.
    		•
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped - Provides a tangy and slightly sweet taste to the curry.
    		•
  • 2 green chilies, slit - Enhances the spiciness of the dish.
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste - Adds depth of flavor and removes any seafood smell.
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder - Helps in removing prawn odors and has health benefits.
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder - Adjust according to spice tolerance.
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder - Adds a mild earthy aroma.
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala - A spice blend that gives an authentic South Indian touch.
    		•
  • Few curry leaves - Adds an aromatic and distinct flavor.
    		•
  • 2 tablespoons oil - Used for frying and bringing out the flavors.
    		•
  • Salt to taste - Enhances the overall flavor.
    		•
Method:
Step 1 - Sautéing onions
Heat oil in a pan, add curry leaves and chopped onions. Sauté until they turn golden brown. This step is crucial as it gives a deep flavor to the curry.

Step 2 - Cooking tomatoes
Add green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, and chopped tomatoes. Cook until tomatoes become soft and mushy, which helps in making a thick gravy.

Step 3 - Adding spices
Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

Step 4 - Cooking prawns
Add cleaned prawns to the mixture and stir well. Cover and cook for 10 minutes until the prawns are fully cooked and coated in the masala. Be careful not to overcook, as prawns can become rubbery.

Step 5 - Serving
Serve hot with steamed rice or roti for a flavorful meal. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves for an extra burst of freshness.
