Gongura Pappu

South Indian Dish

Total Calories: 250 calories
Ingredients:
  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
    		•
  • 2 cups water
    		•
  • 1 cup gongura leaves (chopped, sorrel leaves)
    		•
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
    		•
  • 1 medium tomato (chopped, adds slight sweetness)
    		•
  • 3-4 green chilies (slit, for heat)
    		•
  • 4-5 garlic cloves (chopped, enhances flavor)
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder (adds color and health benefits)
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (adds aroma)
    		•
  • ½ teaspoon mustard seeds (for tempering)
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to spice preference)
    		•
  • 8-10 curry leaves (adds authentic flavor)
    		•
  • 2 tablespoons oil (preferably gingelly or mustard oil for flavor)
    		•
  • Salt as required
    		•
Method:
Step 1 - Cooking Dal
Rinse toor dal thoroughly and pressure cook it with 2 cups of water, turmeric powder, salt, and chopped tomato for about 4-5 whistles until it turns soft and mushy. Mash it well and keep aside.

Step 2 - Cooking Gongura
In a pan, heat 1 teaspoon oil. Add slit green chilies, chopped garlic, and chopped onions. Sauté until onions turn translucent. Then, add the chopped gongura leaves and cook on a low flame until they wilt and soften completely.

Step 3 - Mixing Dal & Gongura
Add the mashed toor dal to the pan with the cooked gongura mixture. Mix everything well and allow it to simmer on low heat for 5 minutes so the flavors blend together.

Step 4 - Tempering
In a small pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil. Add mustard seeds and allow them to splutter. Then, add cumin seeds, curry leaves, and red chili powder. Sauté briefly for a few seconds until fragrant. Pour this tempering over the cooked dal mixture and stir well.

Step 5 - Serving
Gongura Pappu is best served hot with steamed rice and a dollop of ghee. It pairs well with crispy fryums or a side of pickle for an authentic Andhra-style meal.
